A LOOK AT HOW WRIST WATCH WORKS

Before going into working of a wrist watch, let's take a sneak peak into history of a wrist watches !! In starting of 20th century wrist watches have made it's way into market. Mainly they can be classified into following types based on working of showing time and accuracy, they are
• Mechanical watches
• Automatic watches
• Quartz watches
• Atomic watches etc.,.
Out of the above kinds of watch, Mechanical and Quartz type are very much used and made a significant revolution in industry of watch manufacturing industries. So we shall know about them in detail.
Mechanical watches :
They are nothing but mechanical watches whose working was derived mechanically by using different parts like escapement, balance wheel, balance spring, difference wheels connected as gears in order to move one another etc. Balance spring is the main part where movement is produced using a wound spring, wounding and unwounding happens continuously and relative motion is generated there and this motion is transferred to wheels using escape wheel, it will move fourth wheel in such a way that it moves one teeth for one second making it work as the wheel supporting seconds hand. Then this wheel moves another subsequent wheel and so on , using the gears movement and controlling of escape wheel by wound spring, continuous movement of hands of watch is achieved. Compared to electronic movements, mechanical watches are less accurate, often with errors of seconds per day, and they are sensitive to position, temperature and magnetism.They are also costly to produce, require regular maintenance and adjustments, and are more prone to failures. So mechanical system was toppled by quartz movement since it is 10 times more accurate then mechanical watches.
Quartz watches :
quartz watches,, have few or no moving parts, except a quartz crystal which is made to vibrate by the piezoelectric effect. An electric voltage is applied to the crystal, which responds by changing its shape so, in combination with some electronic components, it functions as an oscillator. It resonates at a specific highly stable frequency, which is used to accurately pace a timekeeping mechanism.
Quartz crystal is made in such a way that It resonates at 32768 Hertz or 32.768KHz per second in sine form when applied with certain voltage i.e.,.from cell. So this frequency is then converted into square wave and fed to 15 flip-flops, where every flip-flop will reduce the frequency to half of it's input frequency due to its property of switching and at last we will get 1 Hz frequency per second which is used to maintain time mechanism of the watch. Most quartz movements are primarily electronic but are geared to drive mechanical hands on the face of the watch to provide a traditional analog display of the time, a feature most consumers still prefer. According to display, digital watches uses a segment of LCDs to display time and there consists of two layers of LCD displays which are connected and controlled by an electronic chip, this chip reads frequency of quartz crystal from flip-flops and gives output to LCDs accordingly.

ESSAY ON INVENTIVE, INNOVATIVE, INTERROGATIVE

Engineering is much more than a career choice, it is a way of mind. The ability to use your understanding of the universe and manipulate it into useful tools to benefit humanity is a mindset true intuitive engineers possess and is something I have held within me since birth. From the simple enjoyment of Legos as a kid to assembling my own masterpieces in the arts of furniture and electronics, engineering has engineered my mind.
Starting at the early age of 6 years old, I became obsessed with the fine arts of Lego building. As one might call a master-builder, my young mind would dish aside big manuals for projects made by the Lego Company and craft the product using just the box picture alone. After completion, my young apprentice engineer mind would cut down on the project and reform it into my own designs with only my imagination as a limit. From turning an urban town into a castle to transforming a Star Wars Imperial Warship into a truck, I truly held the intuitive mind of an engineer in development.
As I have developed, I have become more advanced than the simple Lego building. From assembling furniture in my own home by hand to manipulating electronics to my benefits, I have exposed myself to the real world of engineering. Without a strong robotics or engineering class in my school (students view them as “blow-offs” making true want for learning near impossible), I have used my home life to expose myself. Through projects such as building my family’s own outdoor heater by hand to rewiring my own TV entertainment system to run more efficiently and safely, I have gained knowledge in many forms of engineering. My most proud of projects, my bed, is not the average bed. Assembling it by hand at the age of 14, it broke after 3 years. Upon reconstruction, my creative mind not only reinforced the bed to double the weight capacity, but designed and programed a remote control, color changing lighting system under and around the bed for aesthetics. With little help from outside sources, I have profoundly self-taught myself the elegance of engineering.
My mindset as a whole has been shaped into the mind of an engineer’s throughout my development as a student, but now, my future lies ahead of me. As I have recently completed my first year at the University of Oklahoma studying electrical engineering, my mind races with innovation. Ideas flow through, but due to my lack of options, I am at a stand-still. I wish to advance my knowledge in all areas of engineering so that I may encompass my ideas fully.
Ideas I have vary, but usually are related to electrical engineering. For example, I have an idea that works similar to the Bluetooth-tracking service known as Tile. The principal includes a slim device, potentially small enough to fit on a piece of tape, that you place on any item you wish to be able to find easily. The idea is that you can place the tape anywhere, then on an app on your phone, you can name and track the items. The tape only relays information on the distance and direction between the user and the tape. Further, the app would include an augmented reality functionality where the camera opens up and if the tape is shown on the camera view (even when covered by other objects in the area), the app will highlight where the tape is. This would allow you to simply open the app up and find the room an object is in and then where specifically the object is in the room. Some further applications to this could be storage organization. The ability to quite literally find a needle in a haystack with the simple use of augmented reality through a camera lens.
Another idea I have been considering recently, and is entirely experimental, is the idea of electromagnets mimicking muscle contraction. At a simplified scale, a muscle fibers most simple action is contraction. A stimulus is sent throught the muscle through nerves, causing all of the muscle fibers to contract. Now, assuming you could make an array of micro-sized electromagnets, could you potentially recreate a piece of muscle tissue when electricity is ran through the array? This would require precise modeling of the electromagnets but in theory, when electricity is ran through, the electromagnets would all turn on simultaneously and thus pull itself together. Some obvious uses of this would be the extreme improvement of prothsetic devices as well as making robotics all the more realitstic.
As I am still young (19 years of age), I am eager to get a jump on the innovation world. My drive to create is now backed with an understanding of the universe that allows me to bring forth ideas with valid real-world implications. I truly am a born-bred engineer and will keep expanding my horizons on the nature of the universe and how it can be manipulated to improve human life.
